Workload Manager

Background

In line with the new mandate of the institution, the workload policy has been revised to keep up with, new modes of teaching and learning, assessment, research, administration, community engagement and contemporary technical innovation. This policy will be used as a time management tool for academic staff to ensure cost-efficiency and cost-effectiveness in academic performance and delivery. All activities related to academic work identified to be valuable to students and are consistent with institutional mission, are grouped and properly factored to regulate the workload flexibility of individual faculty members. This flexibility is important for recognising the unique missions of the schools and their departments and the differences in mutually agreed upon activities of individuals within departments. This policy is, therefore, revised to provide specific guidelines to the university management and faculty regarding the optimum academic workload for faculty members. It is essential that the Council and stakeholders understand the variety and complexity of academic workloads, while being assured that such workloads represent a positive value for the University of Science and Technology. Therefore, the Council is hereby requested to approve this policy

Rationale

The Polytechnic of Namibia needs a new academic workload policy for the following main reasons:

  1. The Institution has succeeded in getting acceptance in its quest to have the name of the Institution changed from “Polytechnic of Namibia” to “Namibia University of Science and Technology”. Since it is already on the path of transforming to a university of science and technology, the Polytechnic, therefore, needs to give significant prominence to research and scholarship as part of the workload;
  2. The academic workload policy must give recognition to those current staff members who do significantly more in promoting excellence in research, teaching and administration/service;
  3. The new academic workload policy should be reflective of the transformation of the Institution from a polytechnic to a university of science and technology.
  4. The Institution needs to employ an academic workload policy that will provide for the development of a professional academic ethos amongst its academic staff in the three main areas of teaching, research and administration/service.

Definition of Academic Work

Teaching

Teaching activities include:

  • Programme and course development, including development of materials and obtaining professional accreditations,
  • Preparing and conducting lectures, tutorials, demonstrations and workshops and course delivery via alternative modes,
  • Research supervision at undergraduate level and honours,
  • Examination and student assessment,
  • Internal moderation of courses,

Research

Research activities include:

  • Undertaking and publishing research as approved by the Institutional Research and Publications Committee (IRPC),
  • Writing articles and other publications,
  • Scholarship in teaching,
  • Research in teaching, including localised teaching,
  • Undertaking higher research degrees or appropriate qualifications as defined in the Human Resources Code,

Administration/Service

Administration activities may include

  • Programme and Course Coordination (including the coordination of Mathematics courses and writing programmes);
  • Timetabling
  • Examinations Coordination;
  • Invigilation of examinations
  • Formal staff supervision

Community Engagement

Community Engagement activities may include

  • Outreach activities to the community in which the staff is involved.
